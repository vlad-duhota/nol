Sexually Transmitted Diseases in Men

Sexually Transmitted Diseases in Men

Men are particularly vulnerable to STDs. Changing partners often and not using contraceptives only increase the risks. STDs in men are manifested with such adverse effects as rash, pain while urinating, pain in the abdomen area, discharge from genitals, etc.

But the most severe effects of untreated STDs are infertility and erectile dysfunction. Infertility in men is the inability to start pregnancy with a female partner. Usually, it’s because there is not enough or unhealthy sperm. In its turn, erectile dysfunction is the inability to get and keep the erection enough to have complete sexual contact.

Is it Possible to Cure Infertility and Erectile Dysfunction Caused by STDs?

Is it Possible to Cure Infertility and Erectile Dysfunction Caused by STDs?

Both conditions are potentially curable. After the treatment with antibiotics, once an STD is cleared up, men usually recover their physiological functions. Yet, depending on the development stage of an STD and the age of a man, the disease may severely impact their health and significantly weaken fertility and erection for a lifetime.

Dependancy Between STDs and Infertility and Erectile Dysfunction

Dependancy Between STDs and Infertility and Erectile Dysfunction

As mentioned, STDs are the frequent cause of both temporary and permanent infertility and erectile dysfunction. A vivid example is the chlamydia infection. The pathogen infects the prostate gland, leading to prostatitis, which may lead to both infertility and erectile dysfunction. STDs may also damage the epididymis and urethra, leading to difficulty starting pregnancy.

Statistics for Infertility and Erectile Dysfunction in Male US Population

About 9% of the male population are infertile. At the same time, about 30.6% of men report experiencing erectile dysfunction and premature ejaculation. It’s difficult to estimate which percentage of these adverse statistics STDs are responsible for, but it’s also known that about 30% of the male population have ever experienced an STD in their lifetime.

Why Regular STD Testing is Crucial for Men

Why Regular STD Testing is Crucial for Men

As evident, STDs can severely undermine men’s health. So, testing is the key to the timely detection and treatment of the condition. It’s recommended to have planned STD testing twice a year. But it’s also crucial to have urgent STD testing in case of experiencing STD-like symptoms or having unprotected contact with a new partner. This way, the health risks are minimized.
