Understanding STIs

Sexually transmitted infections (STIs) are infections that are passed from one person to another through sexual contact. STIs can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites.

STIs are a serious health concern. If left untreated, STIs can cause long-term health problems, including infertility and even death. STI testing is important for both men and women. STIs can be passed from one person to another through sexual contact, so it is important to get tested if you think you may have been exposed.

There are a variety of STI tests available. Some STI tests are quick and easy, while others require a blood sample or swab. STI tests are usually done at a doctor’s office or STI collection facility such as Alliance Health.

If you think you may have been exposed to an STI, it is important to see a healthcare provider as soon as possible. STIs can be treated with medication. In some cases, STIs can be cured. In other cases, STIs can be managed with medication.

STIs, or sexually transmitted infections, are incredibly common. In fact, STIs are some of the most commonly reported infections in the United States. There are many different types of STIs, and each one can be different in terms of symptoms, treatment, and how it is spread.

Genital Herpes is an infection caused by the herpes simplex virus (HSV). It can be caught through sexual contact and often doesn’t present symptoms. When it does, it can be itching but if you have no signs or symptoms of it, then there's still a chance that your partner could get infected. There hasn't been any cure developed for this condition yet - however, medications will help reduce some nausea and pain associated with contracting Genital Herpes!

There is no cure for genital herpes, but medications can ease symptoms and reduce the risk of infecting others. Condoms also serve as an effective way to prevent spread from one person's infected area onto your uninfected areas!

Most people infected with HSV don't know they have it because they don't have any signs or symptoms or because their signs and symptoms are so mild.

When present, symptoms may begin about two to 12 days after exposure to the virus. If you experience symptoms of genital herpes, they may include:

  • Pain or itching. You may experience pain and tenderness in your genital area until the infection clears.
  • Small red bumps or tiny white blisters. These may appear a few days to a few weeks after infection.
  • Ulcers. These may form when blisters rupture and ooze or bleed. Ulcers may make it painful to urinate.
  • Scabs. Skin will crust over and form scabs as ulcers heal.

During an initial outbreak, you may have flu-like signs and symptoms such as swollen lymph nodes in your groin, headache, muscle aches, and fever.

Hepatitis and Sexual Wellness

The liver is a vital organ that is essential in processing our blood. If the liver becomes inflamed, it can cause swelling and damage to various parts of the body, including muscles throughout your entire digestive system and any other organs you may have included near its proximity, such as the eyes or brain!

There are five major types of viral hepatitis: A (HAV), B, C, D & E which all come from different strains of viruses.

Sexual transmission of hepatitis is possible for all types of the virus. However, sexual transmission is more common for HBV and HCV. To reduce your risk of sexual transmission, use condoms during sex and avoid sharing needles or other drug-injection equipment.

If you think you may have been exposed to an STI, it is important to see a healthcare provider as soon as possible. STIs can be treated with medication. In some cases, STIs can be cured. In other cases, STIs can be managed with medication.

If you think you may have been exposed to any type of hepatitis virus, contact your healthcare provider. There are vaccines available for HAV and HBV. There is no vaccine for HCV, HDV, or HEV. However, there are treatments available that can help manage the symptoms and improve your liver health.

Taking Care of Your Sexual Wellness

Sexual wellness is an integral part of overall health and well-being. Unfortunately, many people do not discuss sexual health or take steps to protect their sexual wellness. This can lead to problems like sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

GET STI Tested Today

There are a few things you can do to protect your sexual health:

  • Get tested for STIs regularly. This will help you catch any infections early and get treatment if needed.

  • Use condoms when engaging in sexual activity. This will help reduce your risk of contracting an STI.

  • Talk to your partner about their sexual history and get tested together. This can help you both make informed decisions about your sexual activity.

Taking care of your sexual health is important for your overall well-being. By getting tested, using condoms, and talking to your partner, you can help keep yourself healthy and reduce your risk of STIs.
